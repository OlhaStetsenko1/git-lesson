# Defect Lifecycle

## Stages of Defect Lifecycle

1. **New**: The defect is identified and logged.
2. **Assigned**: The defect is assigned to a developer for resolution.
3. **Open**: The developer starts working on the defect.
4. **Fixed**: The developer has fixed the defect.
5. **Retested**: The tester retests the defect to verify the fix.
6. **Verified**: The defect is verified and confirmed as fixed.
7. **Closed**: The defect is closed after successful verification.
8. **Reopened**: If the defect persists, it is reopened and the cycle continues.

## Defect Reporting

Defects are reported with the following information:
- Defect ID
- Summary
- Description
- Steps to Reproduce
- Severity
- Priority
- Environment
- Screenshots

## Prioritization and Assignment

Defects are prioritized based on severity and impact on functionality. The roles responsible for assignment include project managers and team leads.

## Resolution and Verification

Developers resolve defects and testers verify the fixes. Tools like JIRA and Bugzilla are used for tracking.

## Closure Criteria

Defects are closed after successful verification and confirmation that the defect no longer exists.

## Roles and Responsibilities

- **Testers**: Identify and report defects, retest fixes.
- **Developers**: Fix defects.
- **Project Managers**: Prioritize and assign defects.

## Tools and Systems

- JIRA
- Bugzilla
