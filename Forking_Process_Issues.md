### Risk Description

**Title:** Forking Process Issues

**Detailed Risk Description:** The process of forking prompts, agents, and datasources may not work as expected, leading to project transfer problems. This risk could hinder the transfer of project components, affecting workflow continuity and team collaboration. The affected features include Forking Prompts, Agents, and Datasources, which are designed to streamline project transfer without the need for export/import processes.

**Risk Category:** Process

**Risk Trigger:** Technology

**Risk Likelihood:** Middle

**Risk Impact:** High

**Responsible Person:** Project Manager

**Treatment/Maintenance Plan:**
1. Conduct regular testing of the forking process to identify and resolve issues early.
2. Implement a robust backup and recovery plan to ensure data integrity during transfers.
3. Provide training to the team on best practices for using the forking features.
4. Establish a clear protocol for reporting and addressing forking issues.

**Risk Strategy:** Mitigate

**Mitigation Plan Actions:**
1. Develop automated tests to regularly check the functionality of the forking process.
2. Create detailed documentation and guidelines for the forking process.
3. Schedule periodic reviews and updates to the forking process based on user feedback and technological advancements.

**Contingency Plan Actions:**
1. Have a manual export/import process in place as a fallback option.
2. Ensure that there is a dedicated support team to handle urgent forking issues.
3. Maintain a log of all forking activities to quickly identify and rectify any problems.
