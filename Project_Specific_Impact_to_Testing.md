### Project and Organization Specifics

This section outlines the key factors and specifics of the project and organization that may influence the test process, both positively and negatively. These factors serve as a reusable reminder to address risks and limitations appropriately.

#### Project Phase
- **Current Phase**: Minimum Viable Product (MVP)
- **Impact on Testing**: The focus will be on comprehensive testing to ensure core functionalities are working seamlessly. This includes functional testing, usability testing, and user acceptance testing to validate the business model with early adopters.

#### Technology Stack
- **Technologies Used**: 
  - Frontend: React.js
  - Backend: Node.js with Express
  - Database: MongoDB
  - Payment Gateway: Stripe
  - Hosting: AWS
- **Impact on Testing**: The technology stack requires specific testing tools and expertise. For example, automated testing for React components, API testing for Node.js endpoints, and integration testing for the payment gateway.

#### Constraints & Assumptions
- **Constraints**: 
  - Budget limitations may restrict the extent of testing tools and resources.
  - Limited availability of test environments.
  - Tight deadlines to launch the MVP.
- **Assumptions**: 
  - Test environments will be stable and available when needed.
  - Requirements will remain stable during the testing phase.
  - Necessary third-party services (e.g., payment gateway) will be available and functional.

#### Critical Success Factors
- **Key Factors**: 
  - Performance benchmarks to ensure the platform can handle expected user load.
  - Security requirements to protect user data and transactions.
  - User acceptance criteria to ensure a seamless user experience.
- **Impact on Testing**: 
  - Performance testing to validate benchmarks.
  - Security testing to identify and mitigate vulnerabilities.
  - User acceptance testing to ensure the platform meets user expectations.

#### Time Zones
- **Team Distribution**: 
  - Development team in the US (PST)
  - QA team in India (IST)
- **Impact on Testing**: 
  - Coordination challenges due to time zone differences.
  - Need for overlapping working hours to facilitate communication and collaboration.

#### Other Influencing Factors
- **Additional Factors**: 
  - Regulatory requirements for e-commerce platforms, such as data protection and payment processing regulations.
  - Market conditions and competition in the e-commerce space.
- **Impact on Testing**: 
  - Compliance testing to ensure adherence to regulatory requirements.
  - Market-driven testing priorities to stay competitive and meet user expectations.
