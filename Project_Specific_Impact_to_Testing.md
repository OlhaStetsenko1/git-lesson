### Project Specific Impact to Testing

This section outlines the key factors specific to the project that may influence the testing process, both positively and negatively. It serves as a reusable reminder to address potential risks and limitations effectively.

#### Project Phase
- **Current Phase**: The project is currently in the Minimum Viable Product (MVP) phase. This means the focus is on delivering a functional product with the core features necessary to meet the primary needs of the users. Testing will prioritize core functionalities and user experience.

#### Technology Stack
- **Frontend**: React.js
- **Backend**: Node.js with Express
- **Database**: MongoDB
- **Hosting**: AWS
- **CI/CD**: Jenkins
- The chosen technology stack may introduce specific testing requirements, such as compatibility testing for different browsers and performance testing for server response times.

#### Constraints & Assumptions
- **Resource Constraints**: Limited availability of testing resources, including personnel and testing environments.
- **Assumptions**: It is assumed that all third-party integrations will function as expected and that the development team will provide timely bug fixes.

#### Critical Success Factors
- **User Acceptance**: Ensuring that the MVP meets the key requirements and expectations of the end-users.
- **Performance**: The application must perform efficiently under expected load conditions.
- **Security**: The application must adhere to security best practices to protect user data.

#### Time Zones
- **Team Distribution**: The development and testing teams are distributed across multiple time zones (EST, PST, and IST). Coordination and communication will be critical to ensure smooth collaboration and timely issue resolution.

#### Additional Considerations
- **Regulatory Compliance**: The application must comply with relevant industry regulations and standards.
- **Test Automation**: Emphasis on automating regression tests to ensure quick turnaround times for testing cycles.
