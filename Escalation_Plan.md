**Escalation Plan**

**Purpose:**
The purpose of the escalation plan is to ensure that any issues that cannot be resolved at the initial level are promptly and effectively escalated to higher levels of authority. This plan aims to minimize the impact of unresolved issues on the project timeline and quality.

**Scope:**
This escalation plan covers all types of issues encountered during the testing phase, including but not limited to technical problems, resource constraints, and communication breakdowns.

**Escalation Levels:**
1. **Level 1:** Initial response by the testing team.
   - Criteria: Issues that can be resolved within the team.
2. **Level 2:** Escalation to the Test Lead.
   - Criteria: Issues that require decisions or resources beyond the testing team’s control.
3. **Level 3:** Escalation to the Project Manager.
   - Criteria: Issues that impact the project timeline or budget.
4. **Level 4:** Escalation to Senior Management.
   - Criteria: Critical issues that threaten the overall success of the project.

- **Testing Team:**

- **Test Lead:**
  - Responsible for addressing issues escalated to Level 2.
  - Contact: [Test Lead Contact Information]
- **Project Manager:**
  - Responsible for handling issues escalated to Level 3.
  - Contact: [Project Manager Contact Information]
- **Senior Management:**
  - Responsible for resolving critical issues at Level 4.
  - Contact: [Senior Management Contact Information]

**Escalation Process:**
1. Identify the issue and attempt to resolve it at Level 1.
2. If unresolved, escalate to the Test Lead (Level 2) with all relevant details.
3. The Test Lead assesses the issue and attempts resolution. If necessary, escalate to the Project Manager (Level 3).
4. The Project Manager evaluates the issue and takes appropriate action. If critical, escalate to Senior Management (Level 4).
5. Senior Management addresses the critical issue and ensures resolution.

**Communication Plan:**
- Immediate notification to the next level upon escalation.
- Regular updates to stakeholders on the status of escalated issues.
- Documentation of all communication related to escalated issues.

**Resolution Timeframes:**
- Level 1: Within 24 hours.
- Level 2: Within 48 hours.
- Level 3: Within 72 hours.
- Level 4: As soon as possible, depending on the severity of the issue.

**Documentation:**
- Record all details of the issue, including steps taken to resolve it at each level.
- Store documentation in the project’s issue tracking system.

**Review and Updates:**
- The escalation plan will be reviewed quarterly.
- Updates will be made as necessary by the Test Lead.
- All changes will be communicated to the team and stakeholders.
