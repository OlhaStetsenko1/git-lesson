## Quality and Acceptance Criteria

This section outlines the standards and benchmarks that the product must meet to be considered acceptable. It includes specific criteria for functionality, performance, security, usability, and compliance. The criteria should be measurable and testable to ensure that the product meets the required quality standards before it is released.

### Functionality Criteria
Define the specific functional requirements that the product must meet. Include details on core features, user interactions, and system integrations.

### Performance Criteria
Specify the performance benchmarks that the product must achieve. Include metrics such as response time, throughput, and resource utilization.

### Security Criteria
Outline the security standards that the product must adhere to. Include requirements for data protection, access control, and vulnerability management.

### Usability Criteria
Describe the usability standards that the product must meet. Include aspects such as user interface design, accessibility, and user satisfaction.

### Compliance Criteria
List the regulatory and compliance requirements that the product must fulfill. Include industry standards, legal regulations, and organizational policies.

Each criterion should be measurable and testable, providing clear benchmarks for acceptance. Ensure that the criteria are aligned with the overall quality objectives of the project.
