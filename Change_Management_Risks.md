### Risk Description for "Change Management Risks"

**Title:** Change Management Risks

**Description:** Risks associated with the complete redesign of Prompts, Datasources, and Agents, which may require significant user adaptation. This redesign could lead to resistance from users, a steep learning curve, and potential disruptions in the workflow. The changes may also necessitate extensive training and support, which could strain resources and affect overall productivity.

**Category:** Process

**Trigger:** Process and Practices

**Likelihood:** High

**Impact:** High

**Responsible Person:** Change Management Lead

**Treatment/Maintenance Plan:**
1. Conduct a thorough impact analysis to understand the extent of changes and affected areas.
2. Develop a comprehensive change management plan, including communication strategies, training programs, and support mechanisms.
3. Engage with stakeholders early and often to gather feedback and address concerns.
4. Implement a phased rollout to allow gradual adaptation and minimize disruptions.
5. Monitor the adoption process and provide continuous support and resources.

**Risk Strategy:** Mitigate

**Mitigation Plan Actions:**
1. Develop detailed documentation and user guides for the new system.
2. Organize training sessions and workshops for users.
3. Establish a dedicated support team to assist users during the transition.
4. Create feedback channels to gather user input and make necessary adjustments.

**Contingency Plan Actions:**
1. Revert to the previous system if the new design causes significant issues.
2. Allocate additional resources to support users struggling with the transition.
3. Extend the transition period to allow more time for adaptation.
